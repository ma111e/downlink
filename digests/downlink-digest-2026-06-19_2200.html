Klue OAuth breach victim list grows as Icarus hackers claim attack

Summary
Klue confirmed attackers used a compromised legacy integration credential to steal OAuth tokens and access connected customers’ Salesforce data. The Icarus extortion group has claimed responsibility, and multiple companies say Salesforce records were stolen while their own platforms and internal systems were not affected.

Klue confirmed a security incident affecting part of its integration infrastructure after unauthorized activity was identified on June 12. CEO Jason Smith said an attacker gained access through a compromised legacy credential tied to an integration service. The attacker used that access to obtain OAuth tokens used to connect Klue with third-party platforms, including Salesforce, and accessed data in a number of connected customer environments.

Klue said it has no evidence that customer content stored directly in the Klue platform was impacted, and that the incident was limited to third-party integrations. The company revoked affected credentials and tokens, removed unauthorized code, disabled impacted integrations, launched an investigation, notified law enforcement, and engaged CrowdStrike. ReliaQuest and Huntress reported that attackers used stolen OAuth credentials tied to Klue integrations to access customer Salesforce environments and steal CRM data.

The Icarus extortion group publicly claimed responsibility on its data leak site and pressured Klue and affected organizations to contact them through Session to prevent stolen data from being leaked. Additional affected organizations named in the article include Recorded Future, Tanium, Jamf, Sprout Social, Gong, and Insurity. Several organizations warned that stolen business contact information could be used in follow-on phishing, social engineering, and extortion campaigns.

  • The incident shows that a compromised legacy integration credential can expose downstream customer environments through OAuth tokens even when the primary vendor platform’s stored customer content is not affected.
  • Affected organizations consistently reported Salesforce data theft without impact to their internal systems or platforms, making third-party SaaS integrations the critical exposure point in this campaign.
  • The stolen data included business contacts, sales communications, pricing information, and other CRM records, which several victims warned could enable follow-on phishing, social engineering, and extortion.
  • The attackers reportedly used generated OAuth tokens and Python scripts to query Salesforce APIs for extended periods, highlighting the need to monitor API activity from third-party integrations for abnormal bulk access.
Key Points
  • Klue confirmed that unauthorized activity discovered on June 12 affected part of its integration infrastructure and allowed an attacker to obtain OAuth tokens used to connect Klue with third-party platforms including Salesforce.
  • Klue said the attacker gained access through a compromised legacy credential associated with an integration service and later accessed data in a number of connected customer environments.
  • ReliaQuest and Huntress reported that attackers used stolen OAuth credentials tied to Klue integrations to access customer Salesforce environments and steal CRM data.
  • The Icarus extortion group publicly claimed responsibility for the Klue incident on its data leak site and pressured Klue and affected organizations to contact them via Session.
  • Recorded Future, Tanium, Jamf, Sprout Social, Gong, and Insurity disclosed they were affected, with most saying Salesforce data was stolen but their platforms, infrastructure, payment information, and internal systems were not affected.
