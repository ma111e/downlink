Android 17 Security and Privacy Overview

Android 17 is scheduled to release next month and introduces a broad range of security and privacy features designed to address device theft, financial fraud, and malicious application behavior. Google is prioritizing both proactive threat detection and reactive device recovery mechanisms across the Android ecosystem.

Banking Scam Call Protections

The update expands protections against scammers who spoof caller IDs to impersonate financial institutions. Android will collaborate directly with banking applications to identify spoofed calls and automatically terminate the connection upon detection. Authenticity verification relies on app-level queries and cross-referencing calling numbers against internal databases provided by partner banks. This verification process is strictly isolated and does not interfere with standard customer communications. The initial banking partners include Revolut, Itaú Unibanco, and Nubank. Although the feature launches with Android 17, Google has committed to backporting the functionality to Android 11 and later versions.

Live Threat Detection and Advanced Protection

Live Threat Detection, an anti-stalkerware capability powered by Play Protect, is being expanded to identify additional abuse techniques. The enhanced system will now detect SMS forwarding misuse, concealed accessibility overlays, applications that hide or alter their icons, and malicious background launches. The Advanced Protection device-level security framework, originally introduced in Android 16, is also being strengthened. New restrictions limit accessibility service access exclusively to apps explicitly labeled as accessibility tools. The update also disables device-to-device unlocking and turns off Chrome WebGPU support. Additionally, Advanced Protection now includes scam detection capabilities for chat notifications.

Device Theft Mitigation and “Mark as Lost”

To increase resistance against physical theft, Android 17 introduces an enhanced “Mark as lost” feature. This function allows users to lock their phone using biometric authentication as an alternative to traditional passcodes or PINs. Thieves will be unable to disable device tracking or regain access even if they possess the device passcode or PIN. Once a device is marked as lost, the Quick Settings menu becomes completely unavailable. The system also forcibly disables WiFi and Bluetooth connections to prevent network reconnection. In select markets including Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, and the United Kingdom, this theft protection feature will be available on smartphones running Android 10 or later.

  • Remote Lock and Theft Detection Lock will be enabled by default on Android 17 devices and select Android 10+ devices in targeted regions.
  • Android 17 reduces the number of allowed PIN or password guessing attempts and increases the delay between failed unlock attempts.
  • Devices running Android 12 or higher will allow users to view the lock-screen IMEI for ownership verification and recovery purposes.
  • Temporary precise-location sharing is introduced, alongside improved location access indicators, usage history, and a new contact picker for granting temporary access to specific contacts.

  • Pixel devices will initially gain verification for official Android builds, supported by a public ledger for authentic Google apps and GMS APIs.

  • Carriers will be able to ship devices with 2G networks disabled by default in regions where legacy cellular networks have been retired.
  • The platform is adding post-quantum cryptography protections to ensure future-proof security.

Other capabilities remain open to OEM adoption, meaning implementation will depend on individual device manufacturer schedules and regional carrier requirements.
