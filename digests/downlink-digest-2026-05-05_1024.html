Lure Construction and Delivery Mechanisms

The phishing emails employed sophisticated, enterprise-style HTML templates with structured layouts and preemptive authenticity statements. These design choices made the messages appear more credible than typical phishing attempts. Display names such as “Internal Regulatory COC” and “Workforce Communications” were used to reinforce the illusion of internal communications. Subject lines included phrases like “Internal case log issued under conduct policy” and “Reminder: employer opened a non-compliance case log.” Messages were dispatched through legitimate email delivery services and featured PDF attachments. These attachments purportedly provided additional information about the conduct review and contained links that initiated the credential harvesting flow.

Attack Chain and Technical Execution

The malicious workflow was designed to bypass automated security defenses and maintain user engagement through multiple stages. Victims were directed through several rounds of CAPTCHA challenges and intermediate pages. These steps served to lend the scheme a veneer of legitimacy while filtering out automated analysis. The final destination varied depending on whether the malicious flow was triggered from a mobile device or a desktop system. Ultimately, the campaign utilized adversary-in-the-middle (AiTM) phishing tactics to harvest Microsoft credentials and authentication tokens in real-time. This technique effectively allowed threat actors to bypass multi-factor authentication controls.

A critical development in the threat landscape involves the weaponization of Amazon Simple Email Service. By leveraging infrastructure that both users and security systems inherently trust, attackers facilitate credential theft via highly legitimate-looking sign-in pages. Campaign Overview

The North Korea-aligned state-sponsored hacking group known as ScarCruft has conducted a supply chain espionage attack against sqgame.net, a gaming platform dedicated to Yanbian-themed games. The campaign leverages a trojanized backdoor named BirdCall to compromise both Windows and Android components of the platform. This operation represents a deliberate strategy to target ethnic Koreans residing in China’s Yanbian region and North Korean defectors crossing the Tumen River. The attack aligns with ScarCruft’s historical focus on North Korean defectors, human rights activists, and university professors.

Malware Capabilities and Evolution

BirdCall is an advanced evolution of the previously known RokRAT malware family. While earlier iterations primarily targeted Windows users, the malware family has been actively maintained and adapted across multiple operating systems, including macOS (CloudMensis) and Android (RambleOn). The backdoor is equipped with standard espionage features, including:

  • Screenshot capture
  • Keystroke logging
  • Clipboard content theft
  • Shell command execution
  • System data gathering

Windows Variant Technical Details

The Windows version of BirdCall is deployed via a multistage loading chain. The initial stage consists of Ruby or Python scripts that download and execute subsequent components. These components are encrypted using computer-specific keys to evade static analysis. The Windows update package delivered a trojanized DLL containing a downloader module. This downloader performs environment analysis by checking for running analysis tools and virtual machine environments. Upon passing these checks, it downloads and executes shellcode that fetches and installs the BirdCall backdoor. Evidence indicates the malicious DLL was distributed at least since November 2024, though the update package has since been cleaned.

Android Variant Technical Details

The Android variant of BirdCall was distributed as poisoned APKs available for download from the compromised platform. The attack selectively targeted Android applications, leaving the Windows desktop client and iOS games intact. The Android backdoor incorporates a subset of the Windows functionality while expanding its data collection scope. It actively harvests:

  • Contact lists
  • SMS messages and call logs
  • Media files and documents
  • Screenshots and ambient audio recordings

Analysis of the malware’s lineage has uncovered seven distinct versions, with the earliest iteration dating back to October 2024.

Command and Control Infrastructure

Both the Windows and Android variants of BirdCall rely on legitimate cloud storage services for command-and-control (C2) communications. This operational security measure helps mask malicious traffic within normal user activity. The Windows variant utilizes Dropbox and pCloud for C2. The Android variant expands this infrastructure to include pCloud, Yandex Disk, and Zoho WorkDrive. Zoho WorkDrive has become an increasingly common presence across multiple campaigns attributed to the threat actor.

The supply chain attack is assessed to be ongoing since late 2024. The exact date of the website breach remains unknown, but poisoned Android APKs began distribution around that time. Two specific Android games hosted on the platform were altered to serve malicious APKs:

  • sqgame.com.cn/sqybhs.apk
