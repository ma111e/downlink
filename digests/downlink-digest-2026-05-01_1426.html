Five Core Go-to-Market Challenges

The article identifies five primary obstacles that prevent MSPs from converting rising demand into profitable revenue.

  • Lack of Client Urgency: 77% of MSPs cite this as a major sales challenge. Technical teams recognize security weaknesses but struggle to translate them into business terms that drive immediate investment. Sellers must reframe security program management around operational continuity, regulatory consequences, and reputational liability to create urgency.
  • Expanded Buying Committees: Cybersecurity purchasing decisions now involve an average of over eight stakeholders, with projections exceeding nine by 2026. These committees include executives, finance leaders, IT personnel, and operations managers who each hold distinct motivations. MSPs must develop tailored discovery frameworks to address the specific concerns of each stakeholder group.
  • Cost Objections: 66% of SMBs identify cost as their primary barrier to adopting stronger security measures. Prospects frequently treat security as a sunk cost rather than a business enabler. Overcoming this requires an objective scoring framework and objection handling that targets the underlying beliefs driving hesitation.
  • Compliance as a Catalyst: Over 56% of new managed security agreements are initiated to satisfy compliance requirements. Deadlines tied to cyber insurance renewals, industry mandates, and state privacy laws create hard timelines that organic sales rarely generate. Providers should position compliance readiness as an entry point while positioning a broader security program as the ultimate goal.
  • Untapped Existing Accounts: Established MSPs often leave substantial revenue on the table by focusing exclusively on new client acquisition. Expanding accounts requires a deliberate, data-driven strategy that treats account management as an ongoing advisory relationship.

Strategic Frameworks for Overcoming Barriers

Addressing these challenges requires a disciplined, systematic approach anchored in actionable processes. MSPs must collaborate with technical experts to translate security findings into client-friendly language that emphasizes risk, operational impact, and business value. Identifying all decision-makers and influencers early allows teams to build consensus through targeted messaging and transparent communication. Presenting security investments in terms of measurable impact, such as reduced incident response times or improved operational uptime, supports faster purchasing decisions. Leveraging sales kits, playbooks, and CRM technology standardizes outreach and ensures smooth handoffs across complex deals. Tracking leading indicators like conversion rates and upsell frequency enables teams to identify pipeline bottlenecks and refine their strategy continuously.
