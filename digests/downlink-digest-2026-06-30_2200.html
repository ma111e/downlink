Anthropic says the U.S. Department of Commerce has lifted restrictions on its two most powerful Claude models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5, and the company will start bringing back access to Fable 5 on Wednesday. Anthropic confirmed that the Department of Commerce has lifted export controls on Claude’s two most powerful models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5. In a post on X, the company said it will begin restoring access to Fable 5 on Wednesday. Anthropic said it will share another update soon and thanked users for their patience, as well as those who worked on redeploying the models.

Mythos 5 will remain exclusive to select companies, even after the export controls were lifted. The article says it is unclear whether Fable 5 will roll out to everyone, including users outside the United States. It also notes that references to KYC were recently spotted on Anthropic’s website, raising concerns that models like Fable could be restricted to users in the United States, at least initially.

In plain words

Anthropic is releasing Claude Sonnet 5, a new version of its AI assistant that can do more complex tasks like planning, coding help, research, document work, and using tools such as browsers and terminals. It is meant to be close in ability to the more expensive Opus 4.8 model while costing less, so people using Claude for work or study may get stronger help without paying as much. Free, Pro, and Max users can access it, and developers using Anthropic’s API also get lower pricing than Opus.

Summary
Anthropic is rolling out Claude Sonnet 5, a cheaper model that brings agentic planning, tool use, coding, and research capabilities much closer to its flagship Opus 4.8. It is available to Free, Pro, and Max users, with introductory API pricing below Opus.

Anthropic is rolling out Claude Sonnet 5, a lower-cost model that the company says is close to its flagship Opus 4.8 model in performance. Anthropic describes Sonnet 5 as “built to be the most agentic Sonnet model yet,” with advanced capabilities such as making plans and using tools like browsers and terminals. The article says these features were previously mostly limited to Opus 4.8, but Sonnet 5 can now do almost everything the flagship model can.

The article says Sonnet 5 is a major upgrade for users who rely on Claude for coding, research, automation, document work, and other multi-step tasks. The author reports that Sonnet 5 feels similar to Opus in planning, tool calling, verifying its own work, and handling requests to fix code. Anthropic says testers described Sonnet 5 as “much more agentic than its predecessors” and noted that it can check its own output without always being explicitly asked.

Anthropic says users can adjust effort levels between Sonnet 5 and Opus 4.8 depending on whether they want lower cost or maximum performance. Sonnet 5 launches with introductory API pricing of \(2 per million input tokens and \)10 per million output tokens through August 31, 2026, rising afterward to \(3 and \)15 respectively. Opus 4.8 is listed at \(5 per million input tokens and \)25 per million output tokens, while Sonnet 5 is available to Free, Pro, and Max subscribers.

  • Capabilities that were previously mostly limited to Opus 4.8, such as planning and tool use, are now available in the lower-cost Sonnet tier.
  • The article’s author found Sonnet 5 useful for understanding large codebases and handling “fix your code” follow-up requests, areas where they say Sonnet 4.6 was weaker.
  • Anthropic is positioning Sonnet 5 as a cost-control option for heavy Claude users who may otherwise exhaust usage faster with more expensive Opus models.
  • Sonnet 5’s introductory API pricing is only available through August 31, 2026, after which both input and output token prices increase.
