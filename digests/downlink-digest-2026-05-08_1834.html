Deceptive Tactics and Payment Mechanisms

The developers behind these applications employed several psychological tricks to extract payments from unsuspecting users. Subscribers were charged fees ranging from approximately \(6 to \)80 to access the promised data. Upon payment, users received entirely fabricated phone numbers and names that were hardcoded directly into the app’s source code. One flagged application attempted to establish institutional credibility by publishing under the developer name “Indian gov.in”. A secondary cluster of apps prompted users to enter their email address for data delivery, yet withheld all information until a payment was processed. Additionally, the apps implemented a behavioral manipulation tactic that triggered a fake notification claiming call history had been emailed to the user when they attempted to exit without paying. Clicking this notification redirected the user directly back to the subscription screen.

Despite their deceptive nature, the applications maintained a remarkably simple user interface and avoided requesting any sensitive device permissions.   • Google Play Store’s official billing system
  • Third-party applications supporting the Unified Payments Interface, including Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm

Refund Policies and Platform Response

Google has since removed the flagged applications from its official storefront. Users who subscribed through the official Google Play billing system are eligible for refunds under Google’s standard refund policies. However, purchases made via third-party payment applications or direct credit card entries cannot be refunded by Google.
