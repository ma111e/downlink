In plain words

A serious flaw in Oracle E-Business Suite is being actively used by attackers to break into Oracle Payments, a part of the software used by organizations to handle payments. The affected versions are 12.2.3 through 12.2.15, and Oracle released fixes last month. This matters because successful attacks can let outsiders take over the payment system, and similar recent Oracle flaws have been tied to data theft and extortion campaigns affecting companies such as Nissan.

Summary
Oracle E-Business Suite flaw CVE-2026-46817 is being actively exploited against Oracle Payments systems, allowing unauthenticated attackers to potentially take over vulnerable instances. Organizations running affected versions 12.2.3 through 12.2.15 should apply Oracle’s May 2026 patches and investigate for possible compromise.

A critical flaw in Oracle E-Business Suite, tracked as CVE-2026-46817, has come under active exploitation in the wild, according to Defused Cyber. The vulnerability has a CVSS score of 9.8 and affects Oracle Payments. The NIST National Vulnerability Database describes it as an easily exploitable issue that allows an unauthenticated attacker with network access via HTTP to compromise Oracle Payments.

The flaw affects Oracle E-Business Suite versions 12.2.3 through 12.2.15, and Oracle shipped patches for it in its Critical Security Patch Update last month. Defused Cyber said it observed an actor exploiting the vulnerability on Oracle E-Business honeypots over the weekend, while also noting that there was no known previous exploitation and no public proof-of-concept code. The article says there are currently no details on how the flaw is being exploited, who is behind the activity, or whether it is part of an opportunistic or targeted campaign.

The article places CVE-2026-46817 in the context of recent attacks against Oracle enterprise software. It notes that CVE-2025-61882 in Oracle E-Business Suite was previously weaponized by threat actors linked to the Cl0p ransomware operation, and that CVE-2026-35273 in PeopleSoft Suite was exploited in ShinyHunters data theft and extortion attacks. Nissan has acknowledged being impacted by exploitation of the PeopleSoft flaw, potentially exposing employee payroll records, bank details, Social Security numbers, and other personal and financial data.

  • Defused Cyber observed exploitation even though it said there was no known previous exploitation and no public proof-of-concept code, indicating attackers may have developed private knowledge of the flaw.
  • The lack of public details about the exploit method or responsible actor makes patching and compromise assessment especially important for affected Oracle E-Business Suite users.
  • The article places CVE-2026-46817 alongside recent exploited Oracle flaws in E-Business Suite and PeopleSoft, showing that major Oracle enterprise applications have been repeatedly targeted in recent data theft and extortion activity.
  • Jake Knott’s comments on the PeopleSoft exploitation emphasize that some recent Oracle-related attacks involved multi-step chains and familiarity with the codebase, rather than only simple one-step flaws.
