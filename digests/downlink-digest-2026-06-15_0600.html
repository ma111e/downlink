The vulnerability has a CVSS v3.1 score of 8.6, requires no authentication or user interaction, and can allow attackers with network access to read arbitrary files, including configuration files, logs, and sensitive application data.

Affected UniFi products include UniFi OS Server versions before 5.1.15, Express line versions before 4.0.15, and UDM family versions before 5.1.15. Ubiquiti published fixed versions in Security Advisory Bulletin 065, making this another example of exposure risk concentrated around management interfaces and edge-adjacent infrastructure.

03 Phishing-as-a-Service, Scam Routing, and Identity-Centric Investigations

Group-IB disclosed fraudulent campaigns targeting users across the Middle East and North Africa through Facebook accounts impersonating politicians, public figures, trusted organizations, and telecom providers. The campaigns were attributed to Sniper Dz, a phishing-as-a-service platform that was taken down in an INTERPOL-led operation last month.

Victims were lured with fake offers such as free mobile internet packages, financial compensation, and government subsidy programs, then redirected through intermediary websites to phishing and traffic monetization infrastructure. The scam pages abused browser notification permissions using a VAPID public key, with Group-IB observing the same key across campaigns impersonating Algerian telecom providers and investment scams across multiple regions.

The operators also used back-button hijacking, tab-under redirections, and a traffic distribution system to send victims toward premium-rate call scams, premium SMS subscription fraud, and investment scams. Separately, Constella described how OSINT teams use identity intelligence to correlate emails, usernames, phone numbers, credentials, social accounts, breach data, infrastructure, and public records to accelerate attribution, exposure analysis, relationship mapping, and investigative triage.
