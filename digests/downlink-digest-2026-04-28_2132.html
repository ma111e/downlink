Vimeo has publicly disclosed that unauthorized actors accessed certain user and customer data as a direct result of a recent security breach at the data anomaly detection company Anodot. According to Vimeo’s initial findings, the compromised databases primarily contained technical data, video titles, metadata, and, in some cases, customer email addresses. The company explicitly clarified that the breach did not expose user-uploaded video content, account credentials, or payment card information. The incident was claimed by the ShinyHunters extortion group, which listed Vimeo on its extortion portal and threatened to leak the stolen data by April 30 unless a ransom was paid. Alongside the data leak threat, the group warned Vimeo to expect several annoying digital problems. ShinyHunters stated that the stolen data originated from Vimeo’s Snowflake and BigQuery cloud instances. The broader Anodot incident involved threat actors stealing authentication tokens and leveraging them to access downstream customer environments, primarily exfiltrating data from Snowflake deployments across multiple organizations. Another confirmed victim of this campaign is game development studio Rockstar Games, where attackers claimed to have stolen over 78.6 million records. In Vimeo’s specific case, the exact volume of exfiltrated data remains unclear because the threat actor did not disclose the amount. Vimeo has since disabled all Anodot credentials and completely removed the service’s integration from its internal systems. The video hosting platform, which serves over 300 million registered users and generates $417 million in annual revenue, confirmed that its core operations remain unaffected. Vimeo is currently conducting a thorough investigation with the assistance of third-party security experts and has formally notified law enforcement authorities. The company has committed to providing further updates if the investigation uncovers significant new information regarding the incident.
