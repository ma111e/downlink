Overview

The FBI, working with Google and Black Lotus Labs, dismantled a large Chinese phishing-as-a-service operation called Outsider Enterprise. The operation used AI and distributed phishing kits to support campaigns that impersonated trusted brands and attempted to steal credit card data and passwords. Outsider Enterprise had been active since at least 2023. Google linked the operation to 9,000 fake websites and more than a million fraudulent URLs. Authorities believe campaigns powered by the service led to the theft of more than 3.8 million credit card records and caused an estimated $1.9 billion in losses.

Operation Riptide and the takedown

The action against Outsider Enterprise was part of the FBI’s broader Operation Riptide, which targets cybercrime activity and infrastructure. The effort had both technical and legal components.

During the technical takedown, the FBI and partners seized multiple pieces of infrastructure and assets, including:

  • Multiple administration servers.
  • A Shopify e-commerce storefront.
  • An account the threat actor used to test the phishing service.
  • Around $100,000 in USDT from Outsider payment wallets.
  • Thousands of phishing domains registered at U.S. providers, which now redirect to an FBI splash page.
  • A Telegram bot linked to Outsider Enterprise that contained information on customers of the phishing service.

Phishing infrastructure and techniques

The article describes Outsider Enterprise as a phishing-as-a-service operation that distributed phishing kits. Google said the network coordinated through Telegram and provided kits that allowed criminals to send fake text campaigns appearing to come from Google and other trusted brands.

The operation used AI in support of phishing campaigns. The campaigns impersonated trusted brands in SMS messages and directed users toward phishing infrastructure. Google said the AI-assisted phishing operation affected hundreds of thousands of users worldwide.

Google reported that, over a two-week period in May, 2.5 million SMS messages were sent to Android users from Outsider Enterprise infrastructure. Android users flagged 55,000 of those messages as fraudulent.

Legal and private-sector response

Google filed a civil lawsuit targeting the operation’s infrastructure. The company is also coordinating with AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon to block fraudulent messages before they reach subscribers.

Google said it is using the case to combine aggressive legal action with collaboration involving federal and state governments. It is also advocating for seven bipartisan U.S. anti-scam bills, including the Stop SCAMS Act.

The article states that the Stop SCAMS Act would require the FBI to lead a coordinated national anti-scam strategy. That strategy would bring together federal agencies, law enforcement, and private companies to better track, disrupt, and prevent fraud and scam operations.

Android protections described by Google

Google said Android users are protected from these threats by AI-powered defenses. The defenses include scam detection on Android that warns users about suspicious calls and messaging protections that block more than 10 billion malicious messages every month.

01 Outsider Enterprise Phishing-as-a-Service Takedown

The FBI, working with Google and Black Lotus Labs, dismantled infrastructure tied to Outsider Enterprise, a Chinese phishing-as-a-service operation accused of running thousands of fraudulent websites designed to steal credit card data and passwords. Google linked the operation to approximately 9,000 fake websites and more than one million fraudulent URLs, illustrating the scale at which the group generated and distributed phishing infrastructure.

Authorities believe campaigns connected to Outsider Enterprise stole more than 3.8 million credit card records and caused an estimated \(1.9 billion in losses. During the disruption, the FBI and partners seized multiple administration servers, a Shopify storefront, a testing account, roughly \)100,000 in USDT, and a Telegram bot associated with the operation. The seized assets point to a mature service model spanning web hosting, testing workflows, payment infrastructure, and operator communications.

Google also filed a civil lawsuit targeting the operation’s infrastructure and said it is coordinating with AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon to block fraudulent messages before they reach subscribers. According to Google, Outsider Enterprise sent 2.5 million SMS messages to Android users over a two-week period in May, and Android users flagged 55,000 of those messages as fraudulent. The reported SMS volume underscores the role of mobile delivery channels in modern phishing operations and the operational emphasis on rapid, high-volume lure distribution.

The reporting characterizes Outsider Enterprise as AI-powered, reflecting the broader trend of fraud services adopting automation and generative tooling to scale phishing content, infrastructure, and campaign management. While the article focuses on the takedown rather than technical indicators, the scope of domains, URLs, SMS traffic, and stolen payment records makes the operation one of the more significant recent examples of phishing-as-a-service disruption.
