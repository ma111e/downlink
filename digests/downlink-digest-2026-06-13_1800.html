Overview

A former IT employee of the Saydel Community School District in Des Moines, Iowa, was sentenced to 21 months in prison for conducting a prolonged cyberattack against the district after leaving his job. The defendant, Ezekiel Dean Potter, 34, previously worked as a senior IT support specialist for the district from May 2022 through April 2023.

According to prosecutors, Potter retained access credentials after his employment ended and repeatedly targeted the district’s systems over the next 21 months. The attacks disrupted classroom operations, deleted accounts, impaired access to educational systems, and caused tens of thousands of dollars in remediation costs.

Defendant and Employment Context

Potter worked for the Saydel Community School District as a senior IT support specialist. His employment lasted from May 2022 through April 2023.

After he left the district, prosecutors said he continued to have access credentials and used them to target district systems. The U.S. government described his conduct in a sentencing memorandum as lasting “over a year and a half.”

Prosecutors’ Description of the Conduct

The government said Potter deleted the district’s Facebook page, stripped employees of access to educational platforms and accounts, and repeatedly tried to reset usernames and passwords for other platforms and accounts.

Prosecutors said the activity caused widespread disruption to the school district. They said the attacks impaired the district’s ability to teach students and resulted in tens of thousands of dollars in remediation costs.

Affected Systems and Services

Court documents described multiple district systems and online services affected by the activity.

The affected or targeted services included:

  • The Saydel Community School District Facebook account.
  • Apple School Manager.
  • District MacBooks and iPads managed through Apple School Manager.
  • GoDaddy and other online services.
  • Schoology.
  • Google administrator accounts.
  • Gmail accounts belonging to current and former district employees.

Apple School Manager Disruption

Prosecutors said Potter targeted the district’s Apple School Manager account after his employment ended. He allegedly deleted user accounts, passwords, phone numbers, billing information, and device management server data.

The article states that this prevented school employees from accessing Apple School Manager. It also disabled management of district MacBooks and iPads for roughly a week while staff worked with Apple to recover access.

Schoology, Google Administrator, and Gmail Activity

Court documents said that in January 2025, Potter accessed the district’s Schoology learning management system through a Google administrator account. He deleted an IT employee’s account, which disrupted teacher access to the platform and affected classes for approximately two hours.

A week later, prosecutors said Potter accessed another administrator account. He then deleted nine Gmail accounts belonging to current and former district employees, including the district’s IT director and superintendent.

Attribution and Investigative Details

Court filings said Potter later switched to using a VPN service after receiving Google security alerts that warned of unauthorized account access.

Federal investigators eventually traced some of the activity to IP addresses associated with Potter’s other employers. Those employers included Casey’s Store Support Center and The Printer Inc.

After Potter left The Printer Inc. in January 2025, prosecutors said he asked a former coworker to retrieve and wipe a USB drive from his desk. The coworker instead turned the USB drive over to investigators.

Investigators allegedly found spreadsheets on the USB drive containing usernames and passwords for Saydel School District accounts and services.

Legal Outcome

Potter pleaded guilty in January 2026 to computer fraud charges under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. He did not enter into a plea agreement.

On June 11, Potter was sentenced to 21 months in prison. He was also sentenced to three years of supervised release.

Supervised Release and Restitution

As part of his supervised release, Potter will be subject to restrictions and monitoring related to employment, finances, and computer systems. The conditions include searches of electronic devices upon reasonable suspicion.

Potter must also pay $59,668.81 in restitution. The restitution is owed to the Saydel Community School District and its insurer, Travelers Casualty and Surety Company, for remediation costs related to the attacks.

Impact on the School District

The article states that the attacks disrupted classroom operations and impaired the district’s ability to teach students. The disruptions included loss of access to educational platforms, deletion of accounts, and temporary inability to manage district Apple devices.

The financial impact described in the article was $59,668.81 in restitution for remediation costs. The affected parties named in connection with restitution are the Saydel Community School District and Travelers Casualty and Surety Company.
